# Finch Recovery Contract — GDD

*Act 2 Journey · The Saturn Pursuit*
*Working title: "Where in the Solar System is Carmen Sedna-Deimos?"*

---

## Premise

Saturn's contract is the **lighter counterweight** to the Jovian Society's moral-extraction pressure. Where Jupiter asks the player to feel complicity, Saturn asks the player to play *detective* across the system. Knives Out after the heaviness — a chase, a thief with a code, a man much older than he appears who can finally be surprised by something.

Saturn is where the aristocracy lives. *Altered Carbon*-style multi-century lifespans. People who have outlived three colonization eras. **Mr. Finch**, ostensibly one of those aristocrats, has just been robbed. He hires the pilot to recover his "crypto wallet."

The thief is **Carmen Sedna-Deimos**. She is competent, theatrical when she chooses to be, and one stop ahead the entire time. The contract is the chase — Saturn → Mars → Venus → Earth → Ceres → Neptune — alternating telescope EVAs and bunker raids, each step yielding a clue to the next.

What the player doesn't know until Neptune:

- The wallet did not just contain crypto. It contained a **complete identity dossier** — the only physical record connecting Mr. Finch's current persona to three previous bird-named personas (Sparrow, Magpie, Owl) and to a fourth and original name underneath all of them: **Harold**, an Apollo-era astronaut presumed killed in the late 20th century.
- Mr. Finch is the **current and last** of these personas. The career that built his Saturn fortune spans three centuries and four legal identities.
- The truth of his survival — the part of his story not in the dossier — is that he was extracted by viroids the second before his Apollo mission killed him. He spent forty years in their stasis. He was returned around 2010. Neither the dossier nor any other record acknowledges the viroids; only Mr. Finch knows that part.
- The wallet *also* contained personal materials accumulated across all four lifespans, including photographs from a brief 2010 reunion with his original lover from before the mission.
- Carmen read the dossier. Returned it intact. Kept no copies. Kept the cash as a recovery fee. She is not a thief in the criminal sense — she's a **fundraiser** for a Neptune scientific commune.
- For the first three stops Carmen is covering her tracks professionally. Around stop 4 she notices the pilot. The second three stops are her *playing* with them — auditioning a possible recruit.
- Carmen does not flee at Neptune. She fights, because she wants to see what the pilot is made of. After threshold-break she stops, returns the dossier, and walks. A few in-game days later an inbox message arrives: a Neptune contract offer, signed by her, in which she names Harold once and explains who Mr. Finch actually is.

The arc ends with the player having quietly become entangled with the only Act 3 faction we hadn't met yet, and having delivered an artifact whose contents they did not read but whose weight they now understand. Mr. Finch got his life history back. Carmen got her recruit. The pilot was used by both, and paid well by one. None of them feel cheated.

## Mr. Finch — The Handler

His current and last persona. He has been Mr. Finch for roughly the last hundred years. Before that he was Owl. Before Owl, Magpie. Before Magpie, Sparrow. Before Sparrow, Harold.

### The full chain

- **Harold ([surname TBD], 1950s-1970s):** Apollo-era astronaut. Mission ended in catastrophe. Officially killed in the explosion. Crewmates reported as recovered; one missing. In the recovery operation transcript, the line "Where is Harold?" appears and is unanswered.
- **Sparrow (~2010s-2050s):** Returns to Earth via a process he understands incompletely. Re-credentials academically with paperwork that is good enough to pass and slightly too tidy to question. Publishes papers on orbital mechanics that contain insights nobody else has yet. Joins the resurgent crewed-spaceflight establishment. The man hiding.
- **Magpie (~2060s-2150s):** Senior administrator. The career years. Climbs through international space governance during the early Mars colonization era. Possibly serves on the committee that authorizes the Phobos mission. The man building.
- **Owl (~2150s-2200s):** The wisdom phase. Drafts the regulatory frameworks of the early Solar Authority. The asteroid claims protocols. The manufacturing licensure rules that the Jovian Society now operates inside (and around). Becomes the sought-after elder consultant of the early-23rd-century space-policy world. The man ruling.
- **Finch (~2200s-present):** Retirement. Smaller in scope than Owl. Quietly senior. Settled at Saturn ringside. Has not surfaced into another career and does not intend to. The man stopping.

Each persona lived a complete public lifespan, retired or "died" at a normal-mortality age, and was succeeded by the next bird. Each one is a real public-record figure with citations, awards, possibly mission patches. The dossier on the stolen drive is the only document in existence that proves all four were the same man.

### What he is, beneath the chain

The viroid extraction is *not* in the dossier. He has never written it down. To anyone except himself, the explanation for his Sparrow-era resurrection is whatever Sparrow's paperwork said. He alone knows what actually saved him: the lattice, forty years of stasis, the moment of being pulled out of the cabin one second before it killed him. He owes them his life. He has spent three centuries trying to figure out what he owes and how to pay it. He has never been able to.

The 1970s lover from before the mission — they had a relationship that the world wouldn't permit publicly. He flew. He died. She mourned. He came back forty years later, briefly, in 2010. He saw her once. She put photographs on his drive. He left, because there was no version of returning to her life that wouldn't destroy it. She died in the next decade or two. The photographs are the only thing he has of her, and they live alongside the dossier on the same physical device.

### Voice character

Composed. Patient. Slightly archaic. The bearing of a man who has constructed a sentence carefully every day for three centuries and now does so without thinking about it.

He is **not amused** in a performance sense. He is *resigned* in a structural sense, and his amusement is the texture of resignation that has been processed but never resolved. *Person of Interest's* Harold Finch in temperament. Vincent from *Gattaca* in cosmology — the trade he made cannot be untraded, except that he didn't even get to make it; the universe made it for him at the moment of his death and did not consult him.

Voice cues:

- Long sentences with conversational asides set off in em-dashes.
- Slightly archaic word choice. *"Madame Sedna-Deimos." "I had not been stolen from in some time." "It is, I confess, clarifying."* (Note: "clarifying" replaces "refreshing" in the rewrite — sharper and more in character.)
- Calls everyone "young." The pilot is "young pilot." Carmen is "our young thief," then "our Carmen" when he forgets himself, then "our Madame" when he composes himself again.
- Never raises his voice. Speaks of the chase the way a man speaks of a chess game he is enjoying losing.
- Says "thank you" once, at the end, and means it more than he's let himself mean anything in a long time.

### What he does NOT say

- Never names what's actually in the wallet. Refers to it consistently as *the wallet*, *the device*, or *the hardware wallet*. The "personal records" line in the intro is the closest he comes to acknowledging the dossier exists, and even there he buries it as an aside.
- Never explains why the authorities cannot be involved. The pilot is meant to construct several plausible reasons by the end of the contract.
- Never asks the pilot to kill Carmen. Lethal force is never sanctioned, and his framing makes it clear that violence would offend him aesthetically and operationally.

### What he DID set up

The contract leans into the implication that Mr. Finch partly engineered the encounter — he suspected the wallet would draw out a specific kind of pursuer; he suspected Carmen would notice the pilot eventually. The closing line is the only tell:

> *"The young woman, Madame Sedna-Deimos. If you should encounter her again — and I suspect you will — please do not mention me. We have, I think, concluded our business. I trust her to have understood what was on the device. I trust you to have not."*

The last sentence is the load-bearing one. It tells the player, in a single line, that *Carmen read the contents and Mr. Finch knows she did and is grateful for her discretion*, and *the pilot did not read the contents and Mr. Finch is grateful for that too*. He is honoring both sides of the unspoken arrangement his contract created.

## Carmen Sedna-Deimos — The Thief

A young woman with an old name. Hyphenated surname suggesting mixed lineage from outer-system bodies — Sedna (TNO) and Deimos (Mars). She is from nowhere and everywhere. She is, importantly, **not a viroid, not a faction operative, not part of the established power structure** the pilot has been working within for two acts.

### Surface character

- **Competent.** She hit Mr. Finch. That alone qualifies her. She stays ahead through six stops without breaking stride.
- **Has a code.** She doesn't kill. She doesn't escalate. The bunkers she hides in are abandoned faction sites overrun by viroids — she's exploiting existing chaos, not creating it.
- **Theatrical when she chooses to be.** First three stops she runs professionally — clean tracks, residual forensic evidence only. Last three stops she leaves *taunts*. Posed photographs. Handwritten datapads.

### Real character

Carmen is the **procurement officer** for a Neptune research commune. She doesn't lead it. She doesn't run it. She does the wet work — heists, chases, negotiations — so the researchers can stay in their labs. The crypto she steals from aristocrats funds vegetable-cloning experiments and 3D-printed meat protocols. The aristocrats can afford the loss. The commune can't afford the absence. She picks marks who won't go to authorities for reasons of their own.

Mr. Finch was a perfect mark — old wealth, old reasons to handle this quietly, and (as she discovered while researching him) a *much* longer history than his public biography indicated. She probably has a list. He had been on it for a while.

### Her research

Carmen's preparation for the heist is the second-most-impressive thing about her, after the heist itself. She traced Mr. Finch backward through Owl, then Magpie, then Sparrow — each persona connected to the next by paperwork seams visible only if you knew to look. On the far side of Sparrow she found the seam that didn't close. On the other side of that seam she found Harold on a 1970s Apollo crew manifest with his name struck through.

She is the only person alive who has done this research. Once she had it, she chose to do nothing with it except return the dossier to the man it documented. Her letter to the pilot at the end of the contract is the only place this knowledge is ever recorded in plain language. After that letter, the knowledge exists in three places: with Mr. Finch, with Carmen, and with the player.

### What changes mid-route

Around stop 3 or 4, Carmen notices the pilot. The exact mechanism doesn't matter (Mr. Finch probably tips her off without realizing — sends a message through a channel she monitors). What matters is the **tonal shift**:

- **Stops 1-3 (Saturn → Mars → Venus):** professional cleanup. Forensic, accidental, off-axis. She isn't performing for anyone yet.
- **Stops 4-6 (Earth → Ceres → Neptune):** participating. Posed photographs. Datapads addressed to the pilot. She's no longer running. She's *waiting*.

The shift is Carmen's **recruitment process** happening on screen. The player won't know that until Neptune, but the visual and tonal change is meant to be felt before it's named.

### The Neptune confrontation

Carmen does not flee at Neptune. The final bunker is hers — fully aware she's being chased, fully prepared. The boss wave is her, fighting solo. Mechanically she's a custom enemy who dodges, returns fire, and does not die. After her HP threshold breaks, she stops, says something specifically calibrated to confirm she's been watching since stop 4, returns the dossier intact, and leaves.

A few in-game days later an inbox message arrives. Carmen offers the pilot Act 3 work. The Neptune commune now has a giver line on the inbox. The pilot has a faction they did not know existed when the contract started.

## The Neptune Commune

Seeded here, expanded in Act 3. Worth getting the shape right now so the contract sets it up correctly.

### Faction identity

- **Voice:** collegial, scientific, slightly exhausted. Researchers who have been working on the same hard problems for years and are happy to brief a new contractor on them. They use first names. They explain their work without condescending. They are, importantly, **cheerful** in a way no other faction in the game is.
- **Politics:** scientific socialist commune. Pooled resources, council-based decisions, no individual property over research output. The aristocracy thinks they're charming. The Society thinks they're a tax problem. The Cinderline doesn't think about them. Carmen thinks they're family.
- **What they're researching:** food crops adapted to outer-system conditions, vegetable cloning at scale, 3D-printed meat protocols, space thermodynamics applied to closed-loop life support. The premise is making outer-planet life *food-independent* from Earth and Jupiter — both of which currently extract from outer-system populations through supply-chain control.
- **Funding:** publicly, donations and a few sympathetic Methuselah patrons. Privately, Carmen's heist proceeds. The commune council disapproves of her methods and quietly relies on them.

### Structural role in the game

- **Faction slot:** Neptune. The last unfilled outer-system seat.
- **Voice slot:** warm-academic. Inverse of Vance Hoyt's corporate-banal. Different register from anything in Acts 1-2.
- **Thematic slot:** the only major faction *building* something. Jupiter takes, Saturn hoards, Cinderline ritualizes, Cowboys partner, Marines defend. Neptune builds.

### Cross-act resonance

- **vs. Jupiter:** the Jovian Society is mass-extracting asteroids; Neptune is researching how to make humanity not need that extraction. Carmen choosing aristocrats as marks instead of the Society is interesting — she could rob Vance Hoyt tomorrow. She doesn't. Why not? *(Answer for Act 3 to play with.)*
- **vs. Cinderline:** both treat life seriously, but Cinderline does it through ritual and Neptune does it through agriculture. Possible Act 3 thread: a Cinderline pilot consulting on closed-loop life support for a Neptune lab.
- **vs. Mr. Finch:** the only Act 2 figure who *knows* about the commune via Carmen, who chooses to fund it indirectly through the heist, and who never says so. With the dossier reframe, this is now even more pointed: Carmen has read his entire life. She is the only person in the universe who has. He has, in effect, become a quiet permanent stakeholder in the commune's continued operation by virtue of the trust she chose to extend him in returning the dossier intact.

## The Wallet and the Dossier

A hardware crypto wallet. Palm-sized chrome-and-glass. The kind of device he held onto for two centuries because the keys still worked.

### What's actually on it

- **Cryptocurrency holdings.** Real. Substantial. 200+ years of compounded value.
- **A complete identity dossier covering Sparrow, Magpie, Owl, and Finch.** Academic credentials. Appointment letters. Committee minutes. Signed regulatory frameworks. Death certificates issued to each prior persona. Personal correspondence from each era. The thread that connects all four into one continuous man.
- **Personal artifacts across centuries.** Photographs from the 2010 reunion with his original lover. The mission patch from the Apollo flight. Letters from wives and partners across the personas — Owl was probably married for forty years to a woman who never knew his real name. Photographs of children he had under those personas, who outlived him as Owl, then died normal deaths thinking their father had also died normally. *Generations* of his own descendants are documented in this dossier, and they don't know about each other because they belong to different "Finches" who never met. The drive is *the only place his family tree is actually whole*.

### What is *not* on it

The viroid extraction. The forty years of stasis. The debt to the lattice. None of that is documented. He has never written it down anywhere. Only Mr. Finch knows.

### Why this is the right McGuffin

- **The crypto framing makes the chase plausible.** Heist plot, fenceable McGuffin, mark-with-leverage logic — Mr. Finch could plausibly hire a contractor for this and the player accepts it without question.
- **The dossier makes the closing devastating.** The pilot delivers an object whose true contents they do not read. They learn what was on it only afterward, from Carmen, in a private letter. By that point Mr. Finch has the device back and is, in his estate, slowly opening it. The pilot ends the contract carrying knowledge they were not paid to have.
- **Carmen's gesture works because of the asymmetry.** She kept the cash. She returned the dossier intact. She kept no copies. She *understood the assignment Mr. Finch couldn't make* and demonstrated her understanding by not exploiting the knowledge it gave her. That's why she'll be a good giver — she sees what people actually want, not what they say they want, and she chooses the ethical answer when it would have been easier to choose the lucrative one.

### What the pilot learns

The pilot may not read the dossier (and the contract design assumes they didn't — Mr. Finch's closing line *"I trust you to have not"* makes this canonical). What they learn is delivered in two staggered messages:

1. **Mr. Finch's completion message** thanks them obliquely and confirms, in one line, that Carmen "understood what was on the device" and the pilot "did not." That tells the pilot, retroactively, that they were carrying something with content. Not what.
2. **Carmen's delayed inbox** explains the dossier — Sparrow, Magpie, Owl, Finch, and Harold — in plain language. She names Harold once. She does not name the viroid origin (because she does not know it). The pilot ends the contract with three of the four bird names and the original underneath them. They learn that they delivered the man to himself.

## The Route

Six stops. Alternating telescope EVAs (clue revealed via image calibration) and bunker raids (clue revealed via end-of-bunker chest). The bimodal tonal shift between forensic and theatrical clues lands at stop 4.

| # | Where | Mechanic | Clue tone | What the pilot finds |
|---|-------|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Saturn** | Telescope EVA | forensic — security still | Carmen leaving Mr. Finch's estate, Mars visible in the frame |
| 2 | **Mars** | Bunker raid | forensic — her own field notes | Datapad: terse abbreviations, "v approach. fence. l/m contact. 72hr window." |
| 3 | **Venus** | Telescope EVA | forensic — Zeppelin Exchange security cam | Carmen at a fence transaction, Earth visible through the Zeppelin window |
| 4 | **Earth** | Telescope EVA | **shift** — posed for the camera | Carmen looking directly at the lens, Ceres reference deliberately framed behind her |
| 5 | **Ceres** | Bunker raid | theatrical — addressed to the pilot | Datapad: handwritten, full sentences, "If you got this far, you're not bad. Try Neptune." Signed C. |
| 6 | **Neptune** | Bunker raid | confrontation | End-of-waves boss fight against Carmen herself. She returns the dossier. Walks. |

### The visual shift at stop 4

The pilot won't be told the shift is happening. The clue images and datapads need to *visibly differentiate*:

- **Stops 1-3 clues:** desaturated, surveillance-grade, off-axis composition. Security-camera timestamps in the corner. Carmen is in the frame but not aware of the camera. Field notes are lowercase, abbreviated.
- **Stops 4-6 clues:** well-lit, deliberate framing, Carmen looking at the camera. Datapads in full sentences. Earth's image lands hardest because it's the first stop *after* she's noticed — she's had time to prepare.

Pilots who clock the shift will feel the chase change character before Neptune. Pilots who don't will get the reveal cleanly at Neptune anyway.

### Why this route, geographically

The route is **not random**. It's Carmen's actual transfer-network. Each stop is a place commune-aligned operators already had access:

- **Mars MMC bunker:** abandoned military training site. Plausible safehouse for a freelancer with the right contacts.
- **Venus Zeppelin Exchange:** Lucas Maverick's market is the most permissive fence in the inner system. Carmen liquidates a tranche of crypto here.
- **Earth:** every contraband route has an Earth leg. Probably a museum or gallery — somewhere a stylish thief would naturally pass through.
- **Ceres mining outpost:** abandoned by its original operators when the asteroid played out. Now it's commune-adjacent infrastructure for moving things between inner and outer system.
- **Neptune:** home.

The route was always going to end at Neptune because Neptune is where she was always going. Mr. Finch probably knew this. He chose not to mention it.

## The Telescope EVA Override

The telescope EVA minigame already exists (four knobs, 95% calibration threshold, image clarifies on completion). The contract reuses it with custom imagery and a clue-reveal layer.

### Override scope

When the contract is active and the current step requires a telescope EVA, the **next EVA mission accepted at the targeted planet** gets reskinned as a Carmen telescope EVA. Specifically:

- A special mission appears on the targeted planet's mission board, posted by the **Mr. Finch giver line**. It is flagged as a contract-only mission and only this contract can see it.
- Mission accepts and runs as a normal EVA-side telescope minigame.
- The image being calibrated is one of three pre-authored clue images (Saturn, Venus, Earth).
- On 95% calibration, image clarifies fully and the next-stop reveal beat plays.
- Other EVA contracts from other givers remain themselves. The override is per-contract-step, not global.

### Clue-reveal beat

When calibration completes, the image clarifies and a small overlay annotates the destination. Spec:

- 1.5s pause where the player can read the clarified image with no UI clutter.
- Overlay slides in: a single line, monospace, lower-third. *"NEXT KNOWN LOCATION: MARS"* (or the relevant planet).
- Overlay holds for 3s, then fades.
- Mission completes normally and the next contract step activates.

The player reads the image *before* the overlay tells them the answer. Pilots who notice the destination in the photo before the overlay confirms it get a small moment of "I saw it" satisfaction. The overlay catches anyone who didn't.

### Production cost

**Three hand-composed clue images** required. See `telescope-clue-images-brief.md` for full art briefs.

## The Bunker Chest as Clue Delivery

Bunker missions are a planned feature; end-of-bunker chests are part of the bunker spec but currently sliced out of slice-1 implementation. **This contract requires the chest mechanic to ship.** Reasonable scope decision: implement chest as part of this contract's PR, since it's needed here and useful generally.

### Chest behavior

- Spawns in the arena after the final wave clears.
- EVA interactable — player walks up, presses **E**, chest opens.
- Contents are a single **datapad** prop the pilot picks up. Reading the datapad triggers the clue-reveal beat.
- For non-contract bunkers (general bunker missions outside this contract), the chest can drop standard tradeable loot per the bunker spec's slice-2 plans.

### Datapad clue-reveal beat

- Picking up the datapad opens a UI overlay styled as a tablet display.
- Datapad text appears character-by-character (or pre-rendered with a brief typewriter effect, whichever is cheaper).
- Datapad text contains the next-stop reveal in-fiction.
- Overlay closes on **E** or after 8s, mission completes, next step activates.

### Two datapads required

**1. Mars → Venus (forensic):**
> v approach. fence. l/m contact. 72hr window.
> 14kg drop @ ZX-3 floor.
> back-channel cleared by 04:00 sat-time.
> burn pad after.

The pilot reads it as field notes and infers Venus + Lucas Maverick. The mission completes and the contract advances. No signature, no flourish.

**2. Ceres → Neptune (theatrical):**
> If you got this far, you're not bad.
> Most peel off after Mars. The Venus stop usually buys them out — Lucas pays better than Mr. Finch does.
> You're still here. That's interesting.
> Try Neptune. Bring whatever you've got.
> — C.

The pilot reads this and knows, viscerally, that the chase changed character somewhere behind them. The mission completes and the contract advances.

## The Neptune Boss Fight

The final bunker is Carmen's. The wave loop runs as standard bunker missions until the **final wave**, which is replaced by a **boss encounter** against Carmen as a custom enemy.

### Carmen as enemy

- **Custom enemy type.** Spawns alone in the final wave. No accompanying mooks.
- **HP pool** sized for a fight that lasts 30-60 seconds at standard difficulty. Not a damage sponge. Not a cakewalk.
- **Behavior:** ranged combat, dodges aggressively (tied to player aim — she sidesteps when targeted), takes cover, returns fire. She uses player-grade weaponry (laser equivalent), not viroid abilities.
- **Mode-swap legibility:** she does not interact with science-mode capture or DAN particles. Laser mode is the only mode that damages her. Drill-mode hits don't register.
- **Audio:** she has voice lines, fired on damage thresholds (75%, 50%, 25%):
  - 75% HP: *"You're persistent. I respect that."*
  - 50% HP: *"This isn't even my best safehouse."*
  - 25% HP: *"Last chance to walk."*

### Threshold-break

When her HP reaches **18%** (tunable) the fight ends regardless of damage dealt. She takes a knee, ceasefire is automatic, the bunker scene transitions to a brief scripted beat:

- Carmen stands.
- Holsters her weapon.
- Walks to the chest position.
- Opens the chest, takes out the wallet, *holds it for a beat looking at it*, places it whole on the chest lid.
- Delivers the closing speech:

> "I noticed you on Earth. Honestly thought you'd peel off after Ceres — most do. The bunker was harder than I needed it to be. That was for me, not for you. Sorry."

> "The cash, I'm keeping. Recovery fee. He can afford it. Everything else on the device is intact. He'll know."

> "See you around."

- Walks out of the arena. Door seals behind her.
- Player picks up the wallet from the chest. Mission completes.

### Why she doesn't die

The contract is a **recruitment audition** disguised as a chase. Killing her would void the recruitment — both narratively (she can't offer the player Act 3 work if dead) and thematically (Mr. Finch would not have hired the kind of pilot who would kill her). The contract is structured so death is mechanically prevented at threshold-break.

If the pilot somehow downs her past the threshold (e.g., burst damage that overshoots the gate), the fight ends at the threshold check and the scripted beat plays regardless. Treat HP-below-threshold as the trigger.

### Player death during the fight

Standard bunker death rules apply (death = mission fail, page reload, restart from giver-planet board). The Neptune fight is the hardest beat in the contract; failure is a real possibility. Restart is permissible because the previous five steps are completed and persisted — the player only re-runs the Neptune stop.

### What changes from previous draft

The previous draft had Carmen extract the photo drive from the wallet, drop the drive, and pocket the wallet itself. With the dossier reframe, she returns **the wallet whole, contents intact**. This is what makes her gesture meaningful — she's confirming with her actions that she did not extract or alter anything. Mr. Finch will, on receiving it, verify the contents are untouched. That verification is what builds the quiet trust that makes the commune's long-term safety in his shadow possible.

## The Closing

Two beats, separated in time.

### Beat 1: Mr. Finch's thanks (delivered immediately)

Returning to Saturn after Neptune triggers the contract completion. Inbox message from Mr. Finch. Voice unchanged from the rest of the contract — patient, slightly amused, but for the first time *quiet* rather than *bored*. He thanks the pilot. He pays the closing reward. He delivers the tell line:

> *"The young woman, Madame Sedna-Deimos. If you should encounter her again — and I suspect you will — please do not mention me. We have, I think, concluded our business. I trust her to have understood what was on the device. I trust you to have not."*

The last sentence does triple duty:

- It confirms Carmen read the contents.
- It confirms the pilot did not.
- It thanks both parties for their respective discretions in a single line of polite distinction.

He doesn't explain. He doesn't have to. The pilot, by this point, knows enough to read the line correctly even if they don't yet know the dossier's specifics.

### Beat 2: Carmen's offer (delayed, 3 in-game days)

A new inbox message arrives. Sender: **Carmen Sedna-Deimos · Neptune Commune**. The message is the first time the commune is named in-fiction.

The message:

- Acknowledges the pilot's competence directly.
- Names what was actually on the wallet — explains the dossier, briefly, in plain language. Names Sparrow, Magpie, Owl, Finch, and Harold. The order, deliberately, is reverse-chronological — she found him by tracing back through the personas, and the letter mirrors that path.
- Confirms she returned the dossier intact and kept no copies.
- Frames Mr. Finch as worthy of respect (*"the man has done more useful work in three centuries than the rest of his peers combined"*) — Carmen's view of him is admiring, not predatory.
- Offers the pilot Neptune-issued contracts. The Neptune giver line opens on the inbox.
- Includes a small one-time credit gift (~3000) "from the commune."

Carmen had to clear it with the commune council before sending. She argued. She won. *That's* why the offer is delayed — not for mystique, for plausibility.

The viroid origin of Mr. Finch's survival is **not** in this letter. Carmen does not know it. Only Mr. Finch does. The pilot ends the contract knowing Mr. Finch's *human* history (four bird-name personas across three centuries plus an Apollo origin) but not the *non-human* substrate beneath it. That gap remains the largest unresolved thread in the worldbuilding and is reserved for Act 3 development.

## Outcome Rewards

Single completion path (no transmit/tamper branch).

- **Step closeouts:** ~1500-4000 credits per step, escalating with stop number.
- **Final step closeout:** 8000 credits.
- **Mr. Finch completion reward:** 2x mission pay multiplier on Saturn-issued contracts.
- **Mr. Finch completion reward:** fast-travel unlocked to Saturn.
- **Mr. Finch completion reward:** flagged faction standing — `finch-recovered`. Reserved for Act 3 references.
- **Carmen delayed inbox:** opens the **Neptune Commune giver line**. Future Neptune missions become available. This is the real long-term reward — Act 3 access.
- **Carmen delayed inbox:** small one-time credit gift (~3000).

### Why no transmit/tamper-style choice

The Jovian arc carried the moral choice. This arc carries the *recruitment*. Player agency in this contract is in *how well they execute*, not in what they decide. A binary moral choice would dilute the chase. The contract is structured so the player is being evaluated; the question isn't what the player chooses but whether they keep up.

1. **Mr. Finch's name structure.** *Mr. Finch* (current and final persona) → preceded by *Owl* → preceded by *Magpie* → preceded by *Sparrow* → original name *Harold*. The contract's `from` field uses *Mr. Finch*. Carmen's letter names the bird chain in reverse (Owl, Magpie, Sparrow) and ends with Harold. The previous bird names appear in Carmen's letter only — never in Mr. Finch's own correspondence, which signs as Mr. Finch throughout.

2. **Apollo-era origin and viroid extraction.** Mr. Finch was Harold, an Apollo-era astronaut presumed killed in a late-20th-century mission catastrophe. He was extracted by viroids in the second before the explosion that killed his crewmates. He spent ~40 years in their stasis. He was returned around 2010. The viroid origin is **not in the dossier** and Carmen does not know it. Only Mr. Finch knows. This is canonical worldbuilding and **does not surface in the contract**; it is reserved as background pressure under his voice and Act 3 thread material.

3. **The McGuffin.** The wallet contains crypto AND a complete identity dossier connecting Sparrow, Magpie, Owl, and Finch as the same continuous man. It also contains personal artifacts across the centuries, including photographs from the 2010 reunion with his original lover. Carmen returns the wallet **whole and intact**. She kept the cash. She kept no copies of the dossier.

4. **Carmen's voice lines (Neptune fight).** Three damage-threshold lines and a closing speech, locked in JSON. The closing speech ends with *"Everything else on the device is intact. He'll know."* This is the line that confirms Carmen's discretion through Carmen's own mouth.

5. **Mr. Finch's tell line.** *"The young woman, Madame Sedna-Deimos. If you should encounter her again — and I suspect you will — please do not mention me. We have, I think, concluded our business. I trust her to have understood what was on the device. I trust you to have not."* The final sentence is the load-bearing addition — it confirms Carmen read the contents and the pilot did not, in a single line of polite distinction.

6. **Threshold-break HP ratio.** **0.18** (18%). Tunable in playtest.

7. **Carmen's research path.** Carmen traced Mr. Finch backward through all four personas to Harold on the Apollo crew manifest. This is referenced in her letter via the reverse-chronological structure of the persona reveal. She is the only person in the universe to have done this research. Her decision to use the knowledge ethically — return the dossier, keep no copies, name Harold only in private to the pilot — is the character beat that earns her recruitment role.

8. **Forbidden 1970s love.** Background only. Not surfaced in this contract. The photographs on the drive are from the 2010 reunion. Carmen's letter mentions the personal materials in general terms but does not specifically name the lover or characterize the relationship's nature.

9. **Neptune Commune leadership.** Council-led. Carmen is procurement, not leadership. The commune council remains an offstage entity throughout this contract. Act 3 may surface a named lead-scientist character (warm-academic voice slot) as a second Neptune giver, but the contract does not require one.

10. **Saturn surveillance image plausibility.** The Saturn telescope EVA image is from Mr. Finch's *own* estate cameras, provided by him at contract acceptance. Carmen knew the cameras existed and chose not to look at them — which, per Mr. Finch's narration, is its own kind of statement. The other two telescope images (Venus and Earth) follow the bimodal-shift logic — Venus is a Zeppelin Exchange floor camera (Carmen unaware), Earth is Carmen-posed.

## Act 3 Threads Seeded Here

- **Mr. Finch's viroid origin.** The largest unresolved thread. Mr. Finch carries a non-public history of viroid stasis and emissary-status that nobody else in the universe knows. The Cinderline would be very interested. The Society would weaponize it. Act 3 may decide whether and how this surfaces.
- **The Cinderline's interest in Mr. Finch.** Cinderline has been searching, possibly for centuries, for evidence of a human who was preserved in viroid contact. They have not connected the dots yet. They might.
- **The Phobos discovery's true origin.** The contract implies, without naming, that Mr. Finch's return from stasis is what set humanity on the path to the Phobos discovery. He may have been deliberately steered by the viroids, or carrying information he didn't fully understand, or both. Act 3 may surface this.
- **The commune as moral counterweight.** Neptune is the only major faction *building* something. The Society liquidates, Cinderline ritualizes, the aristocracy hoards. The commune feeds the outer system. Act 3 contracts from Carmen will surface this and let the player participate.
- **Other off-the-books survivors.** If Mr. Finch's life was extracted by classified arrangement, he may not be the only one. Saturn ringside may be quietly populated with people who shouldn't be alive. This is a worldbuilding seed for elsewhere in the game; Act 3 may exploit it.
